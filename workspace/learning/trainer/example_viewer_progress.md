# Example Viewer Progress

## Session
- Date: example
- Target code: example
- Reveal target: Armen Lighthouse, Brittany, France

## Strongest hits
- Correctly identified a tall vertical human-made structure.
- Correctly perceived hard, cold, heavy, slightly rough material qualities.
- Correctly captured the strong presence of water as functionally important, not merely decorative background.
- Correctly sensed the target as a guidance / supervision point for movement.
- Correctly preserved anomalies such as the upper spike / mast and dark masses in the water, which later matched real structural details and hazards.

## Repeated distortions
- Expanded an isolated object into a broader infrastructure complex.
- Converted sea movement into land transport.
- Assumed too much usable shoreline and too much stable dry ground.
- Treated peripheral functional echoes as if they belonged to the target center.

## AOL tendency
- Narrative completion:
  the Viewer tends to build a full system around a valid core signal.
- Functional inflation:
  a single guiding object becomes a terminal, station, or organized traffic hub.
- Medium substitution:
  water-borne motion is converted into land-based machinery.

## What monitor commands would help next time
- “Test how much dry land is really present.”
- “Check what is under the movement: water, ground, or void.”
- “Describe only the medium of movement, not its purpose.”
- “Separate the central object from anything sensed around it.”
- “Give only new data from the immediate center.”

## What should be trained next
- Isolated structure in water vs harbor infrastructure.
- Ship / wave movement vs land transport movement.
- Small center with strong field vs large physical complex.
- Central target vs side-echo from the environment.

## Suggested micro-exercises
- Run short sessions on:
  - lighthouse on rock
  - bridge vs road
  - solitary offshore structure vs port
  - crowd-wave vs water-wave
- Add one recurring monitor question:
  “Can you walk around it on dry land, or does the ground end quickly?”
- Add one recurring viewer self-check:
  “Am I describing the center, or am I expanding the surroundings?”

## Trainer conclusion
The Viewer captured the core target surprisingly well: vertical structure, man-made character, water relation, and navigational function.
The main issue was not lack of contact, but over-interpretation of the surrounding system.
Training should now focus on medium discrimination, center-vs-environment separation, and control of narrative completion.
