# Example My Corrections

## Session
- Date: example
- Target code: example
- Reveal target: Armen Lighthouse, Brittany, France

## Strong hits
- High, narrow, vertical man-made structure.
- Hard, cold, heavy material with a slightly rough feel.
- Strong water presence around the target, not only as background.
- Functional sense of guidance, supervision, and organized movement.
- Thin upper spike / mast / light-like anomaly above the main structure.
- Dark, heavy points in the water that later matched rocks / underwater hazards.

## Misses / overlays
- I overbuilt the target into a port or terminal.
- I assumed too much land and too much surrounding infrastructure.
- I translated ship movement and wave rhythm into land transport and heavy vehicles.
- I added operators, stations, corridors, and traffic systems on land that were not central to the real target.
- I made the environment feel broader, more developed, and more human-occupied than it really was.

## AOLs
- “Port”
- “Terminal”
- “Land transport corridor”
- “Heavy vehicle route”

## What misled me
- The combination of water + structure + navigation function pushed me toward a harbor narrative.
- Repetitive movement near the target was read too quickly as mechanical land movement instead of sea movement.
- I did not test the amount of real dry land strongly enough.
- I treated surrounding functional echoes as if they were located directly at the center of the target.

## What I should test next time
- If I perceive water and structure together, test whether the structure can be walked around on dry land.
- Ask: after a few steps, is there still ground, or only water?
- Before calling movement “transport”, test whether it has wave pulse, water rhythm, or floating behavior.
- Separate central target from side-echoes coming from the broader environment.
- Leave anomalies untouched longer before translating them into infrastructure.

## New correction rules
- Water + structure does not automatically mean port.
- Navigation function can belong to a single isolated object.
- First test the medium of movement: water, land, air, or mixed.
- First test the amount of land. Do not assume a full shoreline, terminal, or access platform.
- An anomaly may be more truthful than a neat interpretation.
