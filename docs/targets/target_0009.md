# Target 0009

**Target coordinates:** 5783 8383  
**Date of the target:** 9–13 July 2025  

**Target:**  Grunwald Battle Reconstrucion, including the XXVII Reconstruction of Grunwald Battle and the surrounding medieval activities, tournaments, ceremonies, and camp life.

---

## Description

The target is not only the battle reenactment itself, but the full multi-day event space of Dni Grunwaldu, where a large temporary medieval environment is created around the Grunwald battlefield. The area becomes a living historical stage filled with armored reenactors, mounted participants, flags, tents, wooden structures, craft stalls, musical performances, religious services, and a dense program of tournaments and ceremonial events.

The event unfolds over several days, beginning on Wednesday and continuing through Sunday. Each day has its own structure, atmosphere, and set of activities, but all of them are connected by the same medieval theme and the same historical setting.

---

### Spatial and visual characteristics

- **Main event space:** A large open battlefield area used for the central reenactment of the Battle of Grunwald.
- **Tournament grounds:** Separate areas for archery, fencing, mounted combat, and other martial competitions.
- **Camp area:** Temporary medieval-style tents, camp structures, and living-history encampments where reenactors stay and prepare.
- **Craft and market area:** Rows of stalls and workshops where artisans demonstrate historical crafts, sell handmade goods, and present medieval technologies.
- **Ceremonial spaces:** Places for masses, processions, appeals, and ritual gatherings.
- **Cultural stage space:** Areas used for concerts, bard performances, fire shows, and evening entertainment.

The visual field is dominated by armor, shields, banners, lances, flags, horses, tents, and crowds of participants and visitors. The landscape itself remains open and grassy, but for the duration of the event it is transformed into a temporary medieval world.

### Event structure and activities

#### Wednesday
The event opens with active competition.

- archery tournament,
- Grunwald-style palanta competition,
- senior knights’ combat tournament,
- long sword championship,
- women’s combat tournament,
- medieval football tournament,
- evening dance and recreation program.

This first day is strongly competitive and sets the martial tone of the whole event.

#### Thursday
The day begins with a medieval-style mass in Latin. After that, the program continues with more combat and social activity.

- archery tournament,
- one-handed sword combat tournament,
- home brewed drink competition,
- author meeting and book promotion,
- pétanque-style games,
- final rounds of the sword tournament,
- group knightly combat competition.

This day combines religious ceremony, intellectual culture, martial contests, and informal historical recreation.

#### Friday
Friday continues the pattern of combat, scholarship, and public presentation.

- medieval mass,
- spear and shield tournament,
- youth combat eliminations,
- historical chess championship based on medieval treatises,
- Grunwald proclamations and banner presentations,
- archery after the proclamations,
- team combat tournament,
- final duel for the Golden Belt,
- evening vigil and procession to the ruins of the battlefield chapel,
- cultural stage program with music and bard performances.

Friday connects the martial side of the event with ritual, music, and historical display.

#### Saturday
Saturday is the central day of the entire event.

- morning liturgical service,
- pre-battle mass in Latin,
- pétanque final,
- youth combat final,
- Grunwald rally on the memorial hill,
- procession of reenactors onto the battlefield,
- main battle reenactment at 15:00,
- evening concerts and fire show.

This is the climax of the whole festival, when the largest number of participants and spectators gather for the battle itself.

#### Sunday
Sunday closes the event with prayer and liturgy.

- morning prayer service,
- Latin mass in medieval style.

### Functional and social character

The whole event functions as a large-scale historical reenactment and cultural festival. It includes:

- medieval combat and tournaments,
- mounted displays with horses,
- religious ceremonies,
- artisan workshops,
- craft markets,
- music and performance,
- camp life and social interaction,
- educational and commemorative elements.

Visitors can watch, walk through the encampments, observe the armor and clothing, listen to music, buy handmade goods, attend masses, and follow the full sequence of the event from morning to night.

The atmosphere changes throughout the week. At first it is focused on competition and preparation. Later it becomes more ceremonial and public. On Saturday it reaches its peak during the battle reenactment. The entire site works as a temporary historical settlement with its own internal rhythm, hierarchy, and choreography.

---

### Human layer

During the event, the site is populated by participants dressed in historically styled clothing and armor. Knights wear helmets, mail, plate elements, surcoats, shields, and weapons. Mounted participants appear in full martial dress with banners and heraldic colors. Support groups, craftsmen, clergy, and camp participants wear simpler medieval garments such as tunics, dresses, cloaks, belts, and head coverings. The overall visual effect is a layered historical scene, with armored combatants, costumed civilians, and modern visitors sharing the same space.

## Descriptive map of the area - general

### A. General structure

The area forms a large ceremonial battlefield complex with a central open field, a memorial hill, a museum zone, and connecting paths leading through the site.  
It is organized as a historical landscape rather than a normal settlement or urban district.

### B. Communication layout

- **Main axis:**  
  A curved pedestrian and service route connecting the museum area, monument zone, battlefield field, and tournament grounds.

- **Local connections:**
  - access paths around the monument hill,
  - branching routes toward the central battle field,
  - links to the memorial stones and commemorative points,
  - connections toward the tournament area and visitor approach roads.

The layout is functional, ceremonial, and landscape-based rather than urban.

### C. Functional–spatial division

#### 1. Central zone
- open battlefield field,
- main reenactment area,
- broad grassy terrain,
- largest visual and symbolic space.

#### 2. Memorial zone
- monument hill,
- commemorative structures,
- symbolic landmark elements,
- ceremonial focal point of the battlefield.

#### 3. Museum / access zone
- visitor entry area,
- museum building,
- parking and approach infrastructure,
- transition from general access to historical space.

#### 4. Peripheral field zone
- open agricultural land,
- low vegetation,
- tree lines and field edges,
- natural boundary around the event area.

#### 5. Path and circulation zone
- curved pedestrian routes,
- ceremonial movement corridors,
- access lines linking the major event spaces.

### D. Environmental and functional character

- dominant function: historical memory, reenactment, cultural heritage, and ceremonial gathering,
- broad open rural landscape with very low structural density,
- strong emphasis on monumentality and historical symbolism,
- temporary event occupation during the Grunwald celebrations,
- quiet, empty, preparatory character before the arrival of participants.
