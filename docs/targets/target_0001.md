- **Road layout:** A local, unpaved (dirt–gravel) road is visible, without clear curbs or drainage infrastructure. The road serves as an access route to single-family residential properties.

  - plastered façades in light colors,
  - the presence of fences (metal or hedges),
  - individual driveways and home gardens.

- **Terrain:** The terrain is hilly / foothill-like, with visible slopes and scenic exposure toward more distant elevations. Dense forest complexes are present in the background, indicating close proximity to natural areas.

- **Additional infrastructure elements:**
  - roadside markers,
  - single vehicles parked along the road,
  - construction elements (e.g., gravel piles, retaining walls),
  - lack of heavy traffic and lack of typical urban infrastructure (sidewalks, street lighting either unclear or absent in the analyzed frames).

- **Satellite view:** Confirms a dispersed, irregular layout of plots and the blending of built-up areas with greenery. A network of local-character streets is visible.

## Descriptive map of the area

### A. General structure

The analyzed area forms a peripheral local settlement pattern organized around Brzozowa Street, which functions as the main communication axis.

### B. Communication layout

**Main axis:** Brzozowa Street (roughly north–south or slightly offset)

**Local connections:**
- Celna Street – to the west
- Świętowa / Świerkowa Streets – to the east

Roads are local, low-capacity, and serve primarily as access routes.

### C. Functional–spatial division

#### 1. Central zone (Brzozowa axis):
- single-family housing,
- medium and large plots,
- partially undeveloped parcels,
- infrastructure still developing.

#### 2. Western zone (toward Celna Street):
- larger share of green and semi-natural areas,
- more dispersed buildings,
- possible transition into agricultural land or unused terrain.

#### 3. Eastern zone (toward Świerkowa Street):
- more orderly residential development,
- home gardens and hedges,
- presence of service or lodging facilities (e.g., Willa Widokówka Lubawka, ISKIERKA).

#### 4. Northern zone:
- transition into forested and elevated terrain,
- presence of religious and recreational sites:
  - Chapel of the Last Supper
  - Stations of the Cross on Święta Góra

#### 5. Southern zone:
- more developed plot network,
- higher building density,
- better communication links with the rest of the town.

### D. Environmental and functional character

- area with single-family residential function,
- low building density,
- strong integration with the natural landscape,
- absence of high-density urban development.
