# Target 0003

**Target coordinates:** 4838 9922  
**Date of the target:** July 2014  

**Target:** Ambassador Bridge, the bridge crossing connecting Detroit, United States, with Windsor, Canada.

---

## Description

### Spatial and visual characteristics

- **Main object (bridge infrastructure):** A suspension bridge structure with:
  - main cables stretched between pylons,
  - vertical hangers supporting the roadway,
  - a steel side truss providing structural rigidity,
  - a multi-lane asphalt roadway.

- **Road layout and traffic:**
  - two-way traffic with separated lanes,
  - presence of passenger vehicles and trucks used in international transport,
  - distant control infrastructure, including the border inspection zone.

- **Hydrological surroundings:**
  The bridge crosses the Detroit River, which:
  - has considerable width and a calm flow,
  - forms a natural national border,
  - shows varying color tones from greenish to blue.

- **Urban context:**
  - on the Detroit side, industrial and infrastructure-oriented development is visible,
  - on the Windsor side, residential layouts and recreational areas dominate,
  - major road arteries are visible, including connections to Interstate 75.

- **Additional elements:**
  - street lighting mounted on curved arms,
  - safety barriers and railings,
  - road markings and lane lines,
  - a Canadian flag visible in the entry zone.

- **Environmental conditions:**
  - good visibility with partial cloud cover,
  - strong sunlight creating sharp shadows from the structure,
  - no precipitation, with conditions favorable for road traffic.

- **Satellite view:**
  - shows the bridge extending linearly across the river in a near north–south direction,
  - a clearly marked international border near the middle of the river,
  - extensive road infrastructure on the American side, including interchanges and elevated roadways,
  - a more regular grid-like urban pattern on the Canadian side.

## Descriptive map of the area

### A. General structure

The analyzed area is an international transport hub centered on the Ambassador Bridge, which serves as a key road connection between the United States and Canada.

### B. Communication layout

- **Main axis:**  
  The bridge, carrying national and international traffic over the Detroit River and linking the road systems of both countries.

- **Connections on the U.S. side (Detroit):**
  - direct integration with the highway network, including I-75,
  - extensive traffic interchanges,
  - logistics and industrial infrastructure.

- **Connections on the Canadian side (Windsor):**
  - urban street network such as University Ave and Riverside Dr,
  - access to residential and recreational areas,
  - lower intensity of heavy infrastructure.

### C. Functional–spatial division

#### 1. Central zone (bridge):
- transport function for passenger and freight traffic,
- large-scale engineering infrastructure,
- no secondary function beyond traffic handling.

#### 2. Southern / American zone (Detroit):
- dominance of industrial and logistics functions,
- presence of warehouses, terminals, and road infrastructure,
- heavy traffic intensity.

#### 3. Northern / Canadian zone (Windsor):
- residential function, including single-family and urban housing,
- presence of green and recreational areas such as riverside parks,
- integration with the local street grid.

#### 4. Hydrological zone (Detroit River):
- natural barrier and transport corridor at the same time,
- administrative border between two countries,
- no permanent built-up function, mainly environmental.

### D. Environmental and functional character

- dominant function: international transport and logistics,
- environment heavily transformed by human activity,
- high traffic intensity and strong economic importance,
- simultaneous presence of recreational elements on the Windsor side.
