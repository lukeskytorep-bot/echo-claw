## Description

### Spatial and visual characteristics

- **Main object (coastal urban beach environment):** A wide sandy beach stretching along a curved shoreline, with the Atlantic Ocean on one side and a dense line of high-rise buildings on the other.

  **Features:**
  - long, continuous beach frontage,
  - visible surf and breaking waves,
  - open sandy foreground with large recreational use,
  - no natural barrier between the beach and the city, only a narrow coastal strip.

- **Urban surroundings:**
  - dense beachfront development with tall apartment and hotel buildings,
  - modern coastal skyline,
  - a strong contrast between the open sea and the compact city edge,
  - visible mountainous or hilly forms in the distance.

- **Beach activity:**
  - numerous beachgoers scattered along the sand,
  - umbrellas, towels, and leisure groups,
  - informal recreational use of the entire shoreline,
  - strong sense of a public, highly active urban beach.

- **Road and infrastructure context:**
  - a major coastal road runs directly behind the beach,
  - hotel and commercial buildings line the avenue,
  - urban infrastructure is tightly integrated with the shoreline,
  - clear transition from public beach to city blocks.

- **Environmental conditions:**
  - bright daylight,
  - partial cloud cover,
  - strong visibility,
  - calm, typical beach-weather conditions suitable for recreation.

- **Satellite view:**
  - shows a broad curved bay,
  - a dense urban grid immediately inland from the beach,
  - the coastal avenue following the shoreline,
  - iconic beachfront development characteristic of Copacabana.

## Descriptive map of the area

### A. General structure

The target is part of a major coastal urban zone organized around a long beach strip and a parallel boulevard running along the Atlantic edge.

### B. Communication layout

- **Main axis:**  
  Avenida Atlântica, following the shoreline and forming the main coastal road.

- **Local connections:**
  - dense city streets extending inland,
  - hotel and commercial access points,
  - pedestrian access to the beach from the urban grid.

Roads and paths are strongly urban in character and serve both local traffic and tourism.

### C. Functional–spatial division

#### 1. Central zone (beachfront):
- wide sandy recreation area,
- high pedestrian activity,
- open view toward the ocean,
- direct public access.

#### 2. Inland urban zone:
- dense residential and hotel development,
- mixed commercial and service use,
- strong vertical building presence.

#### 3. Coastal transport zone:
- avenue parallel to the beach,
- heavy integration of road and tourism infrastructure,
- clear separation between traffic corridor and beach space.

#### 4. Ocean zone:
- open Atlantic frontage,
- surf zone and wide water expanse,
- dominant environmental boundary of the site.

### D. Environmental and functional character

- dominant function: recreation, tourism, and urban beachfront use,
- high density of built environment immediately behind the shore,
- strong contrast between natural coastal space and metropolitan development,
- iconic city-beach interface with intense public use.
