# Target 0002

**Target coordinates:** 2988 9290  
**Date of the target:** September 2017

**Target:** Petra, Jordan.

---

## Description

### Spatial and visual characteristics

- **Main object (rock-cut architecture):** A monumental façade carved directly into solid sandstone rock, identified as Ad-Deir (the Monastery).

  **Features:**
  - a symmetrical composition with classical architectural elements such as columns, pilasters, and a pediment,
  - very large scale, reaching several tens of meters in height,
  - a central entrance leading into an interior carved into the rock,
  - no free-standing structure, as the object is an integral part of the rock massif.

- **Geological surroundings:**
  - a desert-like environment with a dry, rocky character,
  - dominance of sandstone ranging in color from light beige to red-brown,
  - visible rock layering and surface erosion,
  - no permanent vegetation except for a few isolated shrubs.

- **Space in front of the object:**
  - a broad open courtyard with a natural surface of sand and small stones,
  - no formal paving,
  - presence of tourists, indicating an exhibition and tourism function.

- **Tourist infrastructure elements:**
  - resting areas with benches and tables,
  - covered structures made of wood or light materials,
  - small commercial or service points, such as souvenir sales,
  - presence of transport animals, especially donkeys, used locally.

- **Environmental conditions:**
  - strong sunlight and no cloud cover,
  - high contrast between light and shadow,
  - limited access to natural shade, except from rock walls and occasional trees.

- **Satellite view:**
  - shows a large archaeological complex with a linear layout,
  - a main communication axis identified as Colonnaded Street,
  - structures such as the Great Temple, Temenos Gate, and Nymphaeum,
  - irregular pedestrian paths adapted to the terrain.

## Descriptive map of the area

### A. General structure

The analyzed area is part of a large archaeological complex arranged in a valley-like layout, developed along a natural rock corridor.

### B. Communication layout

- **Main axis:** Colonnaded Street, a historic east–west artery forming the urban backbone of the ancient city.

- **Local connections:**
  - an informal network of pedestrian paths,
  - routes leading to rock-cut structures such as Ad-Deir,
  - no road infrastructure for conventional vehicular traffic.

### C. Functional–spatial division

#### 1. Central zone (urban axis):
- concentration of monumental architectural ruins,
- remains of public and religious structures,
- a partially archaeologically reconstructed layout.

#### 2. Western zone:
- Great Temple complex and Temenos Gate,
- remains of large ceremonial structures,
- higher concentration of architectural relics.

#### 3. Eastern zone:
- area around the Nymphaeum and the further course of the Street of Facades,
- transition into less dense rock-cut development,
- greater presence of natural terrain formations.

#### 4. Peripheral zones (slopes and rock massifs):
- rock-cut objects such as tombs and temples,
- no regular urban layout,
- strong dependence on natural topography.

#### 5. Tourist zone (near Ad-Deir):
- space adapted for tourist movement,
- presence of temporary infrastructure such as commerce and rest areas,
- destination point for pedestrian routes.

### D. Environmental and functional character

- dominant function: cultural heritage and tourism,
- no modern residential development in the analyzed area,
- extreme environment marked by dryness, high temperature, and strong sun exposure,
- landscape of high historical and visual value.
