**Target coordinates:** 9384 8389  
**Date of the target:** September 20, 2020  

**Target:** A fragment of the Niepołomice Forest complex in southern Poland, near the town of Niepołomice.

## Description

### Spatial and visual characteristics

- **Land cover (vegetation):**
  - dominance of deciduous and mixed forest,
  - presence of medium- and tall-height trees with dense stand structure,
  - developed undergrowth, including shrubs, young trees, and low vegetation,
  - a clear boundary between dense forest and open space.

- **Transition zone (ecotone):**
  - visible forest–meadow/field edge,
  - a gentle transition from tall vegetation to grassy open terrain,
  - presence of isolated trees and shrubs along the margin.

- **Open surfaces:**
  - flat meadows or agricultural fields,
  - no buildings in the immediate vicinity,
  - agricultural use or semi-natural open terrain.

- **Communication elements:**
  - a narrow, unpaved path leading into the forest,
  - no formal road infrastructure such as asphalt or signage,
  - character of pedestrian or cycling paths.

- **Anthropogenic elements:**
  - a bicycle left at the edge of the field, indicating recreational use of the area,
  - no permanent infrastructure such as buildings or installations.

- **Environmental conditions:**
  - strong sunlight,
  - clear tree shadows, suggesting a low sun angle and possibly morning or afternoon hours,
  - little to no cloud cover,
  - good visibility.

- **Satellite view:**
  - shows a large, continuous forest complex,
  - fragmentation caused by forest roads and water channels,
  - wetland areas marked as “Błoto” and Wielkie Błoto Peat Bog,
  - a hydrological network of ditches and streams,
  - no intensive urbanization.

## Descriptive map of the area

### A. General structure

The analyzed area forms part of a large forest complex with both natural and managed characteristics, clearly divided into forested and open zones.

### B. Communication layout

- **Main axis:**  
  There is no clear main communication axis in the classical sense. The area is dominated by:
  - forest paths,
  - dirt roads with technical or recreational character.

- **Local connections:**
  - an irregular network of paths crossing the forest complex,
  - connections to the edges of agricultural areas.

### C. Functional–spatial division

#### 1. Forest zone (dominant):
- dense tree cover,
- limited light penetration,
- ecological and forestry function.

#### 2. Transition zone (ecotone):
- boundary between forest and open terrain,
- greater biodiversity,
- presence of access paths into the forest.

#### 3. Open zone (meadows/fields):
- flat, sunlit space,
- possible agricultural use,
- no permanent infrastructure.

#### 4. Hydrological zone:
- wetland and peatland areas (e.g., Wielkie Błoto Peat Bog),
- network of streams and drainage ditches,
- important role in water retention and ecosystem function.

### D. Environmental and functional character

- dominant function: natural, recreational, and partly forestry-related,
- low level of urbanization,
- high ecological value,
- presence of individual users such as pedestrians and cyclists,
- relatively stable environment with minimal infrastructural interference.
